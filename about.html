About

Background & Philosophy

My path has wound through history, computer science, software development, special education, cross-country road trips, and now a middle school classroom where I teach 500+ students a year to think computationally, create responsibly, and ask good questions about the technology that shapes their lives.

I studied History and Computer Science at Hobart College, where I discovered that the most interesting problems sit at the boundary of technical systems and human experience. After graduating I worked as a Solutions Architect at Appway in New York, designing business-process applications and learning how to translate between engineering teams and non-technical stakeholders. A gap year traveling 33 states deepened my sense of curiosity and confirmed that I wanted work centered on people, not just code.

I returned to education, first as a special education instructional assistant, then as the computer science and digital literacy teacher at Robert Adams Middle School in Holliston, MA. I built the full 6–8 CS curriculum from scratch, covering everything from AI ethics to app development to interactive art. My classroom is a place where students build real things, collaborate across differences, and see technology as a tool for expression and community.

What Drives Me

I believe the best learning happens when people feel seen, challenged, and connected to something larger than themselves. Whether I'm designing a curriculum unit, building an interactive PBIS installation, or mentoring a robotics team, I'm always asking: How does this experience feel? Who is it serving? What does it make possible?

Where I'm Headed

I'm looking to bring my blend of teaching, development, and design thinking to a role in instructional design, experience design, or educational technology, at a museum, ed-tech company, architecture firm, or any organization that cares about how people learn and interact with the world around them.

Interests

Human-computer interaction, curriculum design, interactive and generative art, community-centered technology, universal design for learning, creative coding (p5.js, Processing, Arduino).

Outside the Classroom

I play guitar, hike with my dog Jaco, and cook with my partner Sammie. I once hosted a weekly radio show on WHWS 105.7 in Geneva, NY for four years, old habits die hard.
