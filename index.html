Care Garden, An Interactive PBIS Installation

A live, generative art installation built in p5.js and staged in our school lobby during a week of PBIS programming. Students contributed acts of kindness through a kiosk, each one bloomed as a unique flower in a shared digital garden on a large display. Thousands of entries came in over five days, and the lobby became a gathering place. Code as an excuse to gather people around something kind.

About

Background & Philosophy

My path has wound through history, computer science, software development, special education, cross-country road trips, and now a middle school classroom where I teach 500+ students a year to think computationally, create responsibly, and ask good questions about the technology that shapes their lives.

I studied History and Computer Science at Hobart College, then worked as a Solutions Architect at Appway in New York, designing business-process applications and learning how to translate between engineering teams and non-technical stakeholders. After a gap year traveling 33 states and a stint at CompostNow in Raleigh, I returned to education, first as a special education instructional assistant, then as the computer science teacher at Robert Adams Middle School in Holliston, MA, where I built the full 6-8 CS curriculum from scratch.

I believe the best learning happens when people feel seen, challenged, and connected to something larger than themselves. Whether I'm designing a curriculum unit, building an interactive PBIS installation, or mentoring a robotics team, I'm always asking: how does this experience feel? Who is it serving? What does it make possible?
