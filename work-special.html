Active projects at the edge of what I teach, hardware tinkering, multiplayer experiences, and classroom robotics I'm building to stretch myself and my students.

micro:bit v2 3D-Printable Classroom Drones

Hardware · Physical Computing · 3D Printing In Progress

Building a classroom-friendly, 3D-printable drone platform around the micro:bit v2. The goal is a low-cost, block-codable drone students can print, assemble, and program for autonomous flight patterns and basic sensor challenges, bringing aerial robotics into middle school without the price tag or complexity of commercial education kits.

Multiplayer Scratch

Shared Play Experiences In Progress

An experiment in adding real-time multiplayer to Scratch projects so students can build games their classmates can join from other devices. Exploring the intersection of networking, block-based programming, and shared play, and what happens to student motivation when code becomes a social medium.

Remy the Robot

Jetson Orin Nano · Autonomous Robotics In Progress

Remy is a classroom robot built on an NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano, a platform powerful enough to run real computer-vision and ML workloads on-device. The plan is for Remy to become a shared classroom character students can interact with, program, and extend: a physical anchor for AI and robotics units that gives abstract concepts a face (and wheels).
